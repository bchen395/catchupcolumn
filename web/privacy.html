Privacy Policy

Effective July 3, 2026

Last updated July 3, 2026

Catch Up Column ("the app", "we", "us") is a private group newsletter you write together with family and friends. This policy explains what we collect, why, and your choices. We do not sell your data, and we do not use it for advertising or cross-app tracking.

Who this app is for

Catch Up Column is intended for adults (18+) and the family members and friends they invite. It is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from them.

What we collect

We only collect what the app needs to work:

Account information

  • Email address — for sign-in, password reset, and delivering your group's editions.
  • Password — stored only as a salted hash by our authentication provider; we never see it.
  • Display name — shown as your byline to other members of your groups.
  • Profile photo (optional) and short bio (optional).

Content you create

  • Posts (title and body text) and any photos you attach.
  • Groups you create (name, description, optional cover image) and your membership in groups.

Device information for notifications

  • A push-notification token and your device platform (iOS/Android), so we can tell you when a new edition is ready. You can decline or revoke this in your device settings.

Automatically recorded

  • Timestamps for accounts, posts, and editions (when they were created or published).

We do not collect your location, contacts, advertising identifiers, or browsing activity, and the app contains no third-party advertising or analytics SDKs.

How your content is shared

Catch Up Column is private by design. Your posts and photos are visible only to members of the specific group you post them in. Editions are delivered only to the members of that group. There is no public feed and no discovery of your content by strangers.

Service providers (data processors)

We use a small number of trusted providers to run the app. They process your data only to provide their service to us:

  • Supabase — database, authentication, and file storage (your account, content, and photos are stored here). supabase.com/privacy
  • Resend — sends the weekly edition emails. When an edition publishes, the recipient email addresses and the edition content are sent to Resend for delivery. resend.com/legal/privacy-policy
  • Expo / Expo Application Services — delivers push notifications. Your push token and the notification text (e.g. your group's name) pass through Expo's push service. expo.dev/privacy
  • Apple and Google deliver the notifications to your device through their push networks.

How long we keep your data

We keep your account and content until you delete them. Deleting a post removes it. Deleting your account removes your profile and content as described below.

Deleting your account and data

You can permanently delete your account at any time from the app: Profile → Delete account. This removes your profile, your posts and photos, your uploaded images, and your group memberships. If you are the sole moderator of a group, moderation is handed off or the group is removed as part of deletion.

If you cannot access the app, you may request deletion by emailing support@catchupcolumn.com from the email address on your account. See Data Deletion Request.

Your choices

  • Edition emails: every edition email includes a one-tap unsubscribe link, per group. You can also stop email delivery for a group from its settings.
  • Push notifications: turn these off in your device settings at any time.
  • Profile: edit your display name, photo, and bio in the app.

Security

Data is encrypted in transit (HTTPS/TLS). Access to your content is enforced by per-group access rules so that only members of a group can read its posts and editions. No system is perfectly secure, but we work to protect your information.

Changes to this policy

If we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date and, where appropriate, notify you in the app.

Contact

Questions or requests: support@catchupcolumn.com
